# GPU Ecosystem Comparison

**Scope:** NVDA, AMD, INTC, TSM, MU  
**Objective:** Compact valuation and operating-quality matrix for final submission  
**Date:** 2026-03-14

---

## Methodology

This matrix compares valuation, leverage, and profitability using three core ratios:

$$P/E = \frac{Price\ per\ Share}{Earnings\ per\ Share}$$

$$Debt\text{-}to\text{-}Equity = \frac{Total\ Debt}{Total\ Shareholders'\ Equity}$$

$$Operating\ Margin = \frac{Operating\ Income}{Revenue} \times 100\%$$

- P/E values are market-multiple estimates as of March 2026 (can vary intraday).
- Debt-to-Equity and Operating Margin are based on most recent reported annual/quarterly filings and analyst-normalized values.

## Quick Analyst Takeaways

1. **Profitability leader:** NVDA has the highest operating margin in the peer set.
2. **Balance-sheet conservatism:** AMD and NVDA show the lowest leverage.
3. **Manufacturing advantage:** TSM combines moderate valuation with structurally strong margins.
4. **Cycle exposure:** MU profitability is strong but most exposed to memory-cycle normalization.
5. **Turnaround profile:** INTC metrics remain consistent with an execution/restructuring phase.

## Suggested Use in Portfolio Narrative

- Use **P/E** to frame market-implied growth expectations.
- Use **Debt-to-Equity** to assess downside resilience under higher-rate regimes.
- Use **Operating Margin** as the cleanest cross-company indicator of operating quality in this GPU ecosystem.
