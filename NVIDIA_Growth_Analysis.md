## 5. Risk Factors Analysis — AI Supply Chain & TSMC Dependency

*Data extracted from Item 1A, FY2026 10-K (Accession #0001045810-26-000021)*

### 5.1 Supply Chain Concentration Risk

The single most critical systemic risk in NVIDIA's 10-K risk factors is its **dependency on third-party foundries**, particularly TSMC (Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company):

> *"Dependency on third-party suppliers and their technology to manufacture, assemble, test, or package our products reduces our control over product quantity and quality, manufacturing yields, and product delivery schedules and could harm our business."*
> — NVIDIA 10-K FY2026, Item 1A

**TSMC Dependency Breakdown:**

| Risk Factor              | Description                                                    | Severity |
|--------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|----------|
| **Manufacturing Sole-Sourcing** | NVIDIA designs but does not fab. TSMC manufactures 100% of leading-edge chips (N4, N3, N2 nodes) | Critical |
| **Geopolitical Risk**    | TSMC's primary facilities are in Taiwan; cross-strait tensions could disrupt supply | High     |
| **Capacity Allocation**  | During peak demand, TSMC allocates capacity across customers (AAPL, AMD, Qualcomm also compete) | High     |
| **Lead Time Exposure**   | "Long manufacturing lead times and uncertain supply and capacity availability...has led and could lead to mismatches between supply and demand." | High     |
| **CoWoS Packaging**      | Blackwell requires CoWoS-L advanced packaging — a TSMC specialty with limited global alternatives | Medium   |

### 5.2 AI Supply Chain Systemic Risks

| Category                    | Specific Risk                                                                    |
|-----------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **HBM Memory (SK Hynix/Micron)** | Blackwell GPUs use HBM3e; SK Hynix is primary supplier, creating concentration | 
| **Networking (Mellanox/InfiniBand)** | NVIDIA's own acquisition — vertical integration reduces but doesn't eliminate risk |
| **Export Controls**        | BIS regulations restrict H-series and Blackwell exports to China; NVIDIA lost ~10% revenue exposure |
| **Power Infrastructure**   | Data centers require 1–2 MW/rack; energy availability limits deployment speed |
| **OSAT (CoWoS, HBM stacking)** | Advanced Packaging is a bottleneck; capacity at TSMC and ASE Group is finite |
| **Groq IP Licensing**      | FY2026 10-K discloses material, nonrefundable payments for IP licensing that may not yield commercial products |

### 5.3 Regulatory & Export Control Risks (10-K Verbatim)

> *"We are subject to complex laws, rules, regulations, and political and other actions, including restrictions on the export of our products, which may adversely impact our business."*

The introduction of the **Diffusion Rule** (Jan 2025) and prior H100/A100 export restrictions have materially impacted NVIDIA's China revenue, which fell from an estimated ~21% of revenue in FY2023 to ~9.1% in FY2026.

## 7. Analyst Notes & Forward-Looking Framework

### 7.1 Revenue Concentration Risk

NVIDIA's 10-K discloses that **a small number of cloud service providers** (Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta, Oracle) account for a substantial share of Data Center revenue. Hyperscaler capex cycles therefore create significant revenue lumpiness.

### 7.2 Gross Margin Trajectory

The FY2026 gross margin decline (74.9% → 71.1%) was anticipated as Blackwell Ultra ramped with higher COGS from CoWoS packaging and NRE amortization. Consensus expects margins to recover toward **73–75%** in FY2027 as yields improve.

### 7.3 CAGR Forward Scenario

Applying the DCF-informed CAGR framework:

$$Fair Value_{NVDA} = \sum_{t=1}^{n} \frac{FCF_t}{(1+r)^t} + \frac{Terminal\ Value}{(1+r)^n}$$

Where:
- $r$ = Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) ≈ 9–11%
- $FCF$ = Free Cash Flow (estimated $100B+ in FY2026)
- Terminal growth rate $g$ ≈ 4–6% (long-run AI infrastructure spend)
